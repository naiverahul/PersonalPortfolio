Understanding Heaps in Data Structures

May 5, 2025 | C++, Data Structures, Interview

A heap is a specialized binary tree-based data structure that maintains two key properties: shape and heap order. In a max-heap, every parent node’s value is greater than or equal to its children, whereas in a min-heap, every parent’s value is less than or equal to its children.

Array Representation

Heaps are most efficiently implemented using arrays. With 1-based indexing, the root resides at index 1, and for any node at index i:

  • Parent index: floor(i / 2)
  • Left child index: 2 * i
  • Right child index: 2 * i + 1

Heap Sort

Heap sort leverages the heap structure to sort in place with a time complexity of O(n log n).
